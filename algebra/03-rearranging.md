# Rearranging Equations

This workshop focuses on changing the subject unknown variable in an equation.

<div class="instructions" markdown="1">

### Part 1: Two-Step Equations

To solve an equation like $ax + b = c$, you must isolate $x$.
**Rule:** Whatever operation you perform to one side of the equals sign, you must perform to the other.

**Example:** Make $x$ the subject of $ax + 1 = p$

1.  **Eliminate the constant (+1):** Subtract 1 from both sides.

    $$ax = p - 1$$

3.  **Isolate $x$ (remove the $\times a$):** Divide both sides by a.

    $$x = \frac{p-1}{a}$$
    
    **Result:** $x = \frac{p-1}{a}$

### Part 2: Subject on Both Sides

When the required subject appears on both sides of the equation, you must collect them on one side.

**Example:** Make $x$ the subject of $ax - b = cx + d$

1.  **Move the $x$ terms:** Subtract $cx$ from both sides.

    $$ax - b - cx = d$$

2.  **Move the non $x$ terms:** Add b to both sides.

    $$ax - cx = d + b$$

3.  **Factor out the $x$**.

   $$x(a-c)=d+b$$

4.  **Divide both sides by $$a-c$$

    **Result:** $x = \frac{d+b}{a-c}$

</div>

### Part 3: Expressions with Powers and Roots

If an expression involves powers and roots you will need to know the opposite of these.

Opposite of a **square** e.g. $x^2$ is a **square root**, $\sqrt{}$, or you can use the power of $\frac{1}{2}$

Opposite of a **square root** is a **square**

Opposite of a **cube** e.g. $x^3$ is a **cube root**, $\sqrt[3]{}$, or you can use the power of $\frac{1}{3}$

**Example:** Make $u$ the subject of $v^2=u^2+2as$ 

1.  **Work towards $u^2=$:**  Subtract $2as$ from both sides.

    $$v^2-2as=u^2$$
2.  **Square Root:**  Square root both sides.

    $$\sqrt{v^2-2as} = u$$

    **Result:** $u = \sqrt{v^2-2as}$



**Example:** Make $y$ the subject of $5 \sqrt[3]{y+2} = x-m$

1. **Divide both sides by 5**

   $$\sqrt[3]{y+2}=\frac{x-m}{5}$$

2. **Cube both sides**

   $$y+2=\left ( \frac{x-m}{5} \right )^3$$

3. **Subtract 2 from both sides**

   $$y=\left ( \frac{x-m}{5} \right )^3 -2$$

   **Result** $y=\frac{(x-m)^3}{125} -2$
</div>
